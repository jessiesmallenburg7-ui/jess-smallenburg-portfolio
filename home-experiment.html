Expertise

UX Research

I conduct mixed-methods research — including user interviews, usability testing, heuristic evaluations, persona development, journey mapping, and benchmarking — to uncover the needs, pain points, and workflows of patients, clinicians, and caregivers in complex healthcare contexts.

Human-Centered Design

I translate research insights into intuitive, accessible interfaces, workflows, and educational materials that perform reliably under real conditions of care, with particular attention to health literacy, cognitive load, and inclusive design.

Community Health Education

Drawing on my background in community health education and health equity, I bring evidence-based approaches to health literacy, program design, and culturally responsive communication — ensuring digital tools are not only usable but meaningful within the communities they serve.

How I work

My approach

Four principles that guide how I research, design, and evaluate digital health experiences.

  • Evidence-based

    I ground design decisions in published research, primary interviews, and usability evidence—so recommendations are traceable, defensible, and tied to real clinical constraints rather than preference alone.

  • Clinician + patient dual lens

    I design for the people delivering care and the people receiving it. Workflows must reduce clinician burden while remaining clear, respectful, and usable for patients and caregivers.

  • AI transparency

    When AI enters the workflow, I make provenance, confidence, and human review visible. Trust is designed in through verification gates—not assumed after the model generates an answer.

  • Health equity

    I treat literacy, access, culture, and social context as design requirements. Interfaces and content should work for diverse communities, not only for the most digitally fluent users.
